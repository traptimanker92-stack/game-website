Resident Evil

The **Resident Evil** series, created by **Capcom**, first launched in 1996 and quickly became a pioneer of the survival horror genre. The story mainly revolves around dangerous biological experiments and viruses developed by the evil Umbrella Corporation, which turn humans and animals into terrifying monsters. The early games, like **Resident Evil (1996)** and **Resident Evil 2**, focused on slow-paced gameplay, limited ammunition, and puzzle-solving, creating a tense and scary experience. Over time, the series evolved, with games like **Resident Evil 4** introducing more action and a new camera style, which influenced many modern games. Later titles such as **Resident Evil 7: Biohazard** returned to horror roots with a first-person perspective, while **Resident Evil Village** combined horror with action and a deeper storyline. The franchise also includes famous characters like **Chris Redfield**, **Claire Redfield**, and villains like **Albert Wesker**. Apart from games, Resident Evil has expanded into movies, animated films, comics, and novels, making it a huge global franchise. Its mix of horror, action, storytelling, and memorable characters has made it one of the most influential and successful video game series of all time. Origin of Survival Horror: The first game, Resident Evil (1996), is credited with popularizing the term “survival horror,” combining fear, limited resources, and strategic gameplay. Umbrella Corporation: A major part of the story revolves around the evil Umbrella Corporation, responsible for creating dangerous viruses like the T-virus. Different Game Styles: The series has experimented with multiple styles—from fixed camera angles (early games) to third-person shooting (Resident Evil 4) and first-person horror (Resident Evil 7: Biohazard). Remakes Success: Modern remakes such as Resident Evil 2 (2019) and Resident Evil 3 (2020) brought updated graphics, gameplay, and new fans to the series. Iconic Locations: Many games are set in memorable places like Raccoon City, a city destroyed due to a virus outbreak. Puzzle Solving: Along with combat, players must solve puzzles, find keys, and manage inventory, which adds depth to gameplay. Boss Battles: The series is known for intense boss fights against mutated creatures and powerful enemies. Multiplayer & Spin-offs: Besides main games, there are multiplayer and spin-off titles like Resident Evil: Operation Raccoon City.

Minecraft

**Minecraft** is one of the most popular and creative video games in the world, originally developed by **Markus Persson** (also known as Notch) and later expanded by **Mojang Studios**. Released in 2011, Minecraft is a sandbox game that allows players to explore a block-based, open world where they can build structures, gather resources, craft tools, and survive against enemies. The game has different modes, including Survival mode, where players must collect resources and protect themselves from creatures like **Creeper** and zombies, and Creative mode, where players have unlimited resources to build anything they imagine. One of Minecraft’s biggest strengths is its freedom—players can create houses, cities, machines, and even entire worlds. Minecraft also features multiplayer gameplay, allowing friends to join and build together online. Over the years, it has received many updates adding new biomes, mobs, and features. Its simple graphics, combined with deep gameplay and creativity, have made it a favorite among players of all ages, and it remains one of the best-selling video games of all time. One of the most unique features of Minecraft is its open-ended gameplay. There are no strict goals, allowing players to create their own objectives, whether it’s building massive structures, exploring caves, farming resources, or defeating the Ender Dragon. This freedom encourages creativity and problem-solving skills, making the game popular among all age groups. Additionally, Minecraft supports multiplayer mode, where players can join servers and play with friends or other players around the world. This adds a social aspect to the game, where people collaborate to build cities, compete in mini-games, or go on adventures together. The game also has educational benefits, as it is used in schools to teach subjects like coding, mathematics, and teamwork in a fun and interactive way.
